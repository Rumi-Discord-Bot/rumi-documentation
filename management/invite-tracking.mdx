title: "Invite tracking"
description: "Track invite joins, active invitees, vanity joins, leaderboards, and join logs."
icon: "user-plus"
---

Rumi's invite tracker compares Discord invite usage as members join. It records
the inviter when Discord provides enough information and separates normal
invite, vanity, and unknown joins.

## Enable and inspect tracking

```text
-invites enable
-invites settings
```

Changing settings requires **Manage Server**. Use `settings` to confirm whether
tracking is enabled and whether a join-log channel is configured.

## Add a join log

```text
-invites log #join-logs
-invites log off
```

The log channel receives the tracking information Rumi could resolve for new
joins.

## View member and server statistics

```text
-invites
-invites @member
-invites top weekly
-inviter @member
-joins monthly
-vanityjoins alltime
```

The tracker distinguishes total joins, active invitees, members who left,
normal invite joins, vanity joins, and joins whose inviter could not be
determined.

Supported report periods include `daily`, `weekly`, `monthly`, and `alltime`.

## Refresh the snapshot

```text
-invites refresh
```

This asks Rumi to fetch the current invite list again. It refreshes the
comparison snapshot; it cannot reconstruct inviter information that Discord did
not provide for an earlier join.

<Info>
Invite attribution is not guaranteed for every join. Deleted invite links,
vanity links, missing permissions, unavailable cache state, and simultaneous
invite changes can leave a join marked as unknown. `-invites refresh` updates
the snapshot Rumi uses for future comparisons.
</Info>
